# OrbisLoop AI Infrastructure Research

This folder explains how OrbisLoop can evolve from a circular economy platform into an **AI-enabled intelligence layer for reverse logistics, material recovery, and circular supply chain coordination**.

## Why this exists

The public landing page explains the business vision. This folder explains the technical direction behind that vision.

For OrbisLoop to become core infrastructure for the circular economy, the platform must eventually do more than store records and display dashboards. It should help operators answer questions such as:

- What material volumes are likely to appear next week?
- Which pickups should happen first?
- Which routes minimize transport cost and contamination risk?
- Which materials are reusable, recyclable, or contaminated?
- Which partners can absorb forecasted supply most efficiently?
- Where are bottlenecks emerging in recovery operations?

## What is included

- `system-architecture.md` — proposed data and AI system architecture
- `ai-roadmap.md` — staged rollout of intelligence capabilities
- `computer-vision-workflow.md` — example workflow for material image analysis
- `forecasting-routing-simulation.ipynb` — simple simulation notebook for planning and routing concepts

## AI capability themes

### 1. Visibility
Create structured visibility into material generation, movement, processing, and recovery.

### 2. Prediction
Forecast waste generation, pickup demand, contamination probability, and processor capacity constraints.

### 3. Optimization
Recommend collection schedules, route plans, and downstream allocation strategies.

### 4. Automation
Support workflow triggers, anomaly alerts, and eventually semi-autonomous operational decisions.

## Example use cases

- supermarket food waste forecasting
- reverse logistics scheduling for recyclable materials
- contamination detection from photos at pickup points
- processor matching for recovered materials
- route planning for aggregators and fleet operators
- circularity KPI estimation for enterprise reporting

## Status

These documents are **directional and illustrative**. They are meant to demonstrate product and systems thinking, not to claim that every AI capability described here is already deployed in production.
